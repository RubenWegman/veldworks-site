The thesis

The coordination problem has been solved by technology. Organisations continue to pay for it in overhead.

Read the full thesis

The shift

For most of human history, knowledge was trapped in individuals. Writing let it cross time. The printing press let it scale. The internet made it universal. Through all of these breakthroughs, one thing remained constant: a human being had to learn.

The break

AI breaks this entirely. When an AI system learns something, that knowledge propagates instantly. The bottleneck has shifted. The question is no longer how fast knowledge spreads. The question is how fast it is applied.

The implication

Companies were built to solve a coordination problem. No single person could know enough, or do enough, to achieve meaningful scale. That premise is being structurally dismantled. The overhead remains. The necessity does not.

Three irreplaceable elements

The ability to see

Recognising where the opportunity is before the data shows it. Pattern recognition built through years of close observation of how organisations actually work and fail.

The will to act

The entrepreneurial drive that compels you toward a solution. Not a skill. A character trait. This is not something that can be installed through process.

The courage to think differently

The willingness to believe the consensus is wrong. The rarest of the three. It costs something. It cannot be simulated and it cannot be outsourced.
